A. DAILY FLOOR Non-negotiable

(every day, including your worst day)

  • 2 DSA problems — 1 new (or next in sequence), 1 revision-cycle re-attempt. Both logged.
  • 20 min CS fundamentals — active recall only (flashcard review or cold-write), not passive video watching.
  • Any revision reps due today in the spaced repetition tracker (Section D) — non-skippable.
Hitting only the floor = a successful day. The floor exists so a bad day never becomes a zero day. This is the single mechanism protecting you from your documented pattern of stalling out.

B. DAILY TARGET Normal-energy day

  • 4-6 DSA problems (new + revision combined).
  • 45-60 min CS fundamentals (video/reading + active recall write-up).
  • 20-30 min aptitude practice (only during weeks with upcoming OAs — see weekly system).
  • 1 think-aloud recorded problem, 3x/week.

D. RETENTION SYSTEM Fixes core weakness

Every problem you solve — whether independently or with help — enters a tracker with this exact cycle:

Day 1 → Day 3 → Day 7 → Day 21 (cold re-attempt at each interval, closed notes, timer on).
  • Day 1 review: Same day or next day, re-solve from scratch. Catches "I understood it in the moment but didn't encode it."
  • Day 3 review: Re-solve cold again. If you fail, re-derive it, then schedule Day 1 again (reset).
  • Day 7 review: Most forgetting happens by day 5-7. Cold re-attempt, no hints for 15 mins minimum.
  • Day 21 review: Final check — retains pattern long-term, drops from active rotation.
  • Fail at any stage → reset to Day 1. No exceptions, no "I basically remembered it."

Tracking Sheet (One Spreadsheet, 6 Columns)

Problem | Pattern Tag | Date First Solved | Next Review Date | Review Stage (1/3/7/21) | Pass/Fail History

Update it same-day, every day, as part of your floor. This is 3-5 minutes of admin — not optional.

Why this matters: volume without forced cold-retrieval already failed once. Do not repeat that mistake.
